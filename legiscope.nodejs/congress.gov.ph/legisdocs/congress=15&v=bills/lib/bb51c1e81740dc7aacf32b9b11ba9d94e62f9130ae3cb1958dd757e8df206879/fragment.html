      4. Under study by the Technical Working Group(TWG) on 2012-05-22
          -The Technical Working Group (TWG) tasked to consolidate the provisions of House Bill Nos. 2182, 4199, and 4219 re Enhancement of the Basic Education Curriculum or K to 12, held its initial meeting on 22 May 2012 at 9:30 am, Conference Rooms 14&15, RVM Building, House of Representatives, Quezon City. Those in attendance were Reps. Rosenda Ann Ocampo, Mel Senen S. Sarmiento, Mariano U. Piamonte, Jr., Pryde Henry Teves, Magtanggol T. Gunigundo I, Luzviminda Ilagan, Jane T. Castro, Antonio L. Tinio, Thelma Z. Almario resource persons from the Commission on Higher Education (CHED), Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA), Department of Education (DepEd), Catholic Educational Association of the Philippines (COCOPEA), Philippine Association for State Universities and Colleges (PASUC), Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE), Philippine Regulation Commission (PRC) and the secretariat of both Committees. The TWG agreed on the following: • Section 1 - Short Title, was adopted as presented • Section 2 - Declaration of Policy, was adopted with amendments. DepEd will include in the provision the rationale on the need to change the education cycle • Section 3 – Definition of Terms, was adopted with the following amendments: - Deletion of the word ‘compulsory’ in the definition of secondary education - revise the wordings of the definition of secondary education - provide for the definitions of junior high school, senior high school, alternative learning systems, out-of-school youth, adult learners, and education for those with special needs • The DepEd was instructed to submit these definitions to the CBEC secretariat on Friday, 25 May 2012 • Section 4 – Enhanced Basic Education Program, was adopted with the amendments by CHED, to include in the provision the use of Mother Tongue as the medium of instruction for grades 1 to 3 • Rep. Gunigundo was assigned to reword Section 4 • DepEd, CHED, TESDA, DOLE, PRC, PASUC and COCOPEA to submit to the Committee their comments on the proposed substitute bill • Section 5 was adopted with the following amendments: - provision of basic standards to serve as guide for DepEd in laying the foundation for curriculum development - creation of a Curriculum Advisory Council

Mitra Jr. Bldg., House of Representatives, Quezon City to discuss and finalize the revised substitute bill on House Bill Nos 2182, 3479, 4199, 4219 and House Resolution No. 12 entitled, “AN ACT ENHANCING THE PHILIPPINE BASIC EDUCATION SYSTEM BY STRENGTHENING ITS CURRICULUM AND INCREASING THE NUMBER OF YEARS FOR BASIC EDUCATION”, introduced by Reps. F. Belmonte, Escudero, E. Magsaysay, Teodoro, Palmones, et al. Participants:  Rep. Mariano U. Piamonte, Jr. – Presiding Officer  Rep. Salvador H. Escudero III  Rep. Mel Senen Sarmiento  Rep. Ma. Theresa Bonoan-David  Atty. Tonisito Umali – Assistant Secretary, DepEd  Atty. Antonio Paredes – Office of ASec. Umali, DepEd  Mr. Sinforoso M. Birung – Director IV, CHED  Mr. Nap B. Imperial – Deputy Executive Director, CHED  Ms. Maria Susan P. Dela Rama – Director, Planning Office, TESDA  Atty. Joseph Noel M. Estrada – Executive Director, COCOPEA  Mr. Ariodear Rico – President, PAPRD, Professional Regulation Commission (PRC)  Ms. Luzviminda S. Guzman – Chair, Board of Guidance and Counseling, PRC  Mr. Dominador Aquino – Bureau of Local Employment, DOLE  Mr. Benjamin Valbuena – President, Manila Public School Teachers Association Agreements Made: • The body adopted Section 1 of the reference substitute bill. • On Section 2, paragraph 3 of the substitute bill, the body agreed to delete references to “Washington Accord” and “Bologna Process” and in lieu thereof, inserted the phrase “international agreements and standards”. • On Section 3, Definition of Terms 1) The body preferred to delete the separate definitions in items “(h) Junior High School” and “(n) Senior High School” and agreed to just integrate the definitions of these terms in the term “Secondary Education”; 2) The body adopted the definitions presented in the position paper of Rep. Gunigundo, namely; Formal Education, Kindergarten Education, Elementary Education, and Secondary Education, with an amendment that in the definition of Secondary Education, the terms “junior secondary” and “upper secondary” be replaced with “junior high school” and senior high school”, respectively; 3) The body agreed to let the DepEd do the final refinements of the terms defined in Section 3. • On Section 4, Enhanced Basic Education Program 1) The body agreed to delete paragraph 2, 4 and 5; 2) A provision from the position paper of Rep. Gunigundo was inserted as the last paragraph of Section 4, to wit: “Students of Filipino nationality enrolled in all types of public schools operating under the enhanced basic education program shall be free from payment of tuition and other school fees. Learners of non-Filipino citizenship shall be required to pay the necessary fees as specified by the DepEd.” • On Section 5, Curriculum Development 1) The body agreed to replace the second sentence of paragraph 1 with the following: “To ensure college readiness and to avoid remedial and duplication of basic education subjects, the DepEd shall coordinate with CHED and TESDA. 2) An amendment was likewise agreed upon to include the PRC among the agencies to be consulted in the development of the curriculum. • The body adopted Section 6 with an amendment that the curriculum consultative committee may only recommend to the DepEd the formulation of necessary refinements in the curriculum. • The body agreed to replace the entire provision of Section 7 with the following: “SEC. 7. Integration of General Education Curriculum Subjects. – In addition to the general education curriculum (GEC) subjects that may hereafter be determined by the DepEd as part of the new curriculum, subjects mandated by existing laws and currently being offered in tertiary programs shall be incorporated in the secondary education curriculum • Section 8, Hiring of Graduates of Science, Mathematics, Statistics and Engineering 1) The body agreed to insert a preliminary statement at the start of the paragraph, to wit: “Notwithstanding the provisions of Sections 26, 27 and 28 of Republic Act No. 7836, otherwise known as the Philippine Teachers Professionalization Act of 1994,”; 2) The body agreed to delete the second sentence of the paragraph starting from the word “Provided” up to the word “LET”, subject to style. • On Section 9, the body agreed to insert a preliminary statement at the start of the paragraph, to wit: “Notwithstanding the provisions of Sections 26, 27 and 28 of Republic Act No. 7836,”. • The body agreed to insert a new Section 10, to wit: “SEC. 10. Hiring of Higher Education Institution Faculty. – Notwithstanding the provisions of Sections 26, 27 and 28 of Republic Act No. 7836, the faculty of higher education institutions (HEIs) shall be allowed to teach general education subjects in the secondary education: Provided, That the faculty must be a holder of a relevant Masters degree: Provided, further, That the faculty must have satisfactorily served for at least five (5) years of full-time service as such faculty.” • Section 10, Career Guidance Counseling Advocacy 1) Section 10 was re-numbered as the new Section 11; 2) The body agreed to revise the second sentence, to wit: “Notwithstanding the provisions of Section 27 of Republic Act No. 9258, otherwise known as the Guidance and Counseling Act of 2004, career and employment guidance counsellors, who are not registered and licensed guidance counsellors, shall be allowed to conduct career advocacy activities to secondary level students.” • The body adopted Section 10 of the reference substitute bill and re-numbered it as the new Section 11. • Section 11, Expansion of E-GASTPE Beneficiaries 1) Section 11 was re-numbered as the new Section 12; 2) The body agreed to adopt the new Section 12 with an amendment to delete the phrase, “subject to the availability of funds”. • The body adopted Section 12 of the reference substitute bill and re-numbered it as the new Section 13. • Section 13, Transitory Provisions 1) Section 13 was re-numbered as the new Section 14; 2) The second sentence, paragraph 3 was deleted and substituted with the following: “The faculty of HEIs allowed to teach students of secondary education under Section 10 hereof, shall be given priority in hiring for the duration of the transition period.” • The body adopted Sections 14, 15, 16 and 17 and re-numbered the same accordingly as Sections 15, 16, 17 and 18. • The body finally agreed to submit the final TWG output for consideration and approval in a joint committee meeting on 5 June 2012.
