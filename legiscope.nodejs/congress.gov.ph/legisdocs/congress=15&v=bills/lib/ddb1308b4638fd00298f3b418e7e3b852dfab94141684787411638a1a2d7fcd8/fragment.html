PRINCIPAL AUTHOR/S : RODRIGUEZ, RUFUS B.
DATE FILED : 2010-08-09
SIGNIFICANCE: NATIONAL
CO-AUTHORS :
1. Rodriguez (M.)
ACTIONS TAKEN BY THE COMMITTEE
COMMITEE HEARINGS/ACTIONS:
      1. Scheduled for first meeting/hearing on 2011-02-02
          -A Technical Working Group was created to deliberate on the eight similar measures on Cybercrime with HB 167 of Rep. Eric Owen Singson, Jr. to be used as the working draft.
      2. Under study by the Technical Working Group(TWG) on 2011-02-14
          -The TWG agreed to adopt the following changes to the working draft on the Cybercrime bill: 1. reword some definitions with those used in the Budapest Convention. 2. a provision on creating a Joint Congressional Oversight Committee was approved by the Body. 3. reword the provisions on content-related offenses particularly cybersex and child pornography. 4. adopt the provision on Conspiracy to commit Cybercrime. 5. insert a new provision defining serious offenses that would merit the interception of data.
      3. Recommendations submitted by the TWG on 2011-05-18
          -The TWG agreed to adopt the following amendments to the bill on Cybercrime: 1. amendments to Sec. 2. Declaration of Policy, Sec. 6. Liability under other laws, Sec. 10 Real Time Collection of Computer Data, Sec. 11. Preservation of Computer Data, Sec. 12. Disclosure of Computer Data and Sec. 13. Search, Seizure and Examination of Computer Data. 2. inclusion of a provision on Cyber Defamation. 3. amendment to Sec. 5. Other Offenses. 4. amendments to Sec. 16. General Principles Relating to International Cooperation, Sec. 17. Applicability of the Convention on Cybercrime, Sec. 18. Department Of Justice and Sec. 19. Commission on Information and Communications Technology. 5. paraphrase Sec. 18. Jurisdiction to add new Sec. 25. Suppletory Provision. 6. include the DILG in Sec. 24. Implementing Rules and Regulations.
      4. Approved by the Committee on 2011-05-30
          -The Substitute Bill was approved with the following amendments: 1. paraphrase the provision on jurisdiction on Sec. 14. 2. reasons should be made clear and should be limited to threats to life and threats to national security on Sec. 13. 3. delete the words "in exchange for a fee paid via credit cards or any other means" in the definition of Cybersex on Sec. 4. 4. add and define the word "Cyber" on Sec. 3.
      5. Scheduled for first meeting/hearing on 2011-11-16
      6. Approved by the Committee on Appropriations on 2011-11-16
          -Substitute Bill approved with amendment.
